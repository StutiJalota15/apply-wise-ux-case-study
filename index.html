ApplyWise • UX Case Study

Designing a job-search career operating system (B2B2C)

ApplyWise helps early-career job seekers track applications, prepare for interviews, and recover constructively from rejection — with a system foundation that can scale to career services and hiring ecosystems.

Role: UX / Product Design
Platform: Responsive Web App
Tools: Figma
Read the case study Jump to carousel

Overview

Job seekers apply to dozens of roles across job boards, emails, and notes. This fragmentation causes missed follow-ups, scattered interview prep, and emotional burnout after rejection.

ApplyWise reframes the job search as a pipeline + support system — optimizing for clarity, emotional safety, and long-term progress.

What I designed

  • Kanban pipeline dashboard for instant status clarity
  • Minimal “Add application” flow to reduce friction
  • Application detail with timeline, notes, and actions
  • Rejection reflection loop to prevent drop-off

The problem

Human

Overwhelm tracking many applications + emotional fatigue from rejection.

System

Job boards focus on listings; ATS serves recruiters; spreadsheets are manual.

Opportunity

Design a pipeline that reduces cognitive load and supports resilience.

System view (B2B2C)

ApplyWise is designed as a three-layer system: job seeker experience today, career services enablement next, and future alignment with hiring ecosystems.

Process

Design goals

  • Reduce cognitive overload with a clear mental model
  • Make “next action” obvious at every stage
  • Support emotional resilience after rejection
  • Keep UI calm, minimal, and accessible

Constraints

  • Concept prototype (no backend)
  • No time for full usability testing
  • Design decisions grounded in common job-seeker patterns

Key decisions

Kanban pipeline (not a list)

Job search is movement through stages. Grouping reduces scanning effort and makes stuck points visible.

Rejection as a reflection loop

Rejection isn’t an edge case — it’s common. Designing recovery prevents drop-off and supports learning.

Calendar tied to status

Interviews and follow-ups create anxiety when disconnected. Pipeline + time view reduces misses.

Minimal “Add application” form

Lower friction increases consistency. Details can be added later (progressive enrichment).

Key screens

Swipe or use arrows to browse. Each image is cropped to fit the card cleanly.

Impact & learnings

Impact (concept metrics)

  • Faster “time to first application” via minimal onboarding
  • Higher follow-up completion via calendar + reminders
  • Better re-engagement after rejection via reflection loop

What I learned

  • Emotional safety is a UX requirement in stressful journeys
  • Systems thinking turns screens into a product narrative
  • Clarity beats feature volume for sustained behavior
