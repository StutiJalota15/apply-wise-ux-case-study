ApplyWise • UX Case Study

Designing an emotion-aware career management platform (B2B2C)

ApplyWise helps early-career job seekers track applications, prepare for interviews, and recover constructively from rejection — with a system foundation that can scale to career services and hiring ecosystems.

Role: Product Designer (UX, Systems & Strategy)
Platform: Responsive Web App
Tools: Figma • UX Pilot • Accessibility
Read the case study Key screens

Overview

Students and early-career professionals apply to dozens of roles across job boards, email threads, notes, and calendars. This fragmentation causes missed follow-ups, scattered interview prep, and emotional burnout after rejection.

ApplyWise reframes the job search as a pipeline + support system — optimizing for clarity, emotional safety, and long-term progress.

What I designed

  • Kanban pipeline dashboard for instant status clarity
  • Minimal “Add application” flow to reduce friction
  • Interview scheduling + prep notes tied to each application
  • Rejection reflection loop to prevent drop-off
  • Resources that support key moments (interview, follow-up, rejection)

The problem

Human

Overwhelm from tracking many applications, deadlines, and prep notes across tools — plus emotional fatigue from rejection.

System

Job boards focus on listings, ATS tools serve recruiters, and spreadsheets are manual — leaving job seekers unsupported.

Opportunity

Design a pipeline that reduces cognitive load, supports emotional recovery, and can scale into a career services platform.

System view (B2B2C)

ApplyWise is designed as a three-layer system: job seeker experience today, career services enablement next, and future alignment with hiring ecosystems.

Job Seeker layer

Pipeline tracking, calendar, interview prep, reflection — a personal “career OS” designed for clarity and emotional safety.

Career Services layer (future)

Opt-in visibility for advisors: bottlenecks, cohort insights, timely guidance — enabling scalable support.

Hiring ecosystem layer (future)

Conceptual integration with ATS stages, interview scheduling, and feedback loops — reducing fragmentation.

Process

Design goals

  • Reduce cognitive overload with a clear mental model
  • Make “next action” obvious at every stage
  • Support emotional resilience after rejection
  • Keep UI calm, minimal, and accessible

Constraints

  • Concept prototype (no backend)
  • No time for full usability testing
  • Design decisions grounded in common job-seeker behavior patterns

Key decisions

Kanban pipeline (not a list)

Job search is movement through stages. Grouping reduces scanning effort and makes stuck points visible.

Rejection as a reflection loop

Rejection isn’t an edge case — it’s common. Designing recovery prevents drop-off and supports learning.

Calendar tied to status

Interviews and follow-ups create anxiety when disconnected. The pipeline + time view reduces misses.

Minimal “Add application” form

Lower friction increases consistency. Details can be added later (progressive enrichment).

Impact & learnings

Impact (concept metrics)

  • Faster “time to first application” via minimal onboarding
  • Higher follow-up completion via calendar + reminders
  • Better re-engagement after rejection via reflection loop

What I learned

  • In stressful journeys, emotional safety is a UX requirement
  • Systems thinking turns “screens” into a product narrative
  • Clarity beats feature volume for sustained behavior
